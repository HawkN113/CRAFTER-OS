# 🛠 CRAFTER Team Adoption Roadmap
> **Goal:** Transition from fragmented execution to a high-performance, self-improving engineering culture.

---

## 📍 Phase 0: Alignment & Preparation (1–2 weeks)
*Objective: Build the conceptual foundation and secure "Buy-in".*

- [ ] **Core Presentation:** Introduce the [CRAFTER Principles](./CRAFTER.md). Focus on "What's in it for the engineer" (less burnout, more flow).
- [ ] **Assign Champions:** Identify 1-2 engineers per squad to act as "System Architects" for the rollout.
- [ ] **Environment Audit:** Identify the top 3 organizational "Noise" sources (e.g., meeting bloat, Slack culture).

---

## 🏃 Phase 1: Tactical Execution (1–2 months)
*Objective: Stabilize daily output and protect engineering bandwidth.*

- [ ] **The Playbook Rollout:** Teams start using the [Daily Checklist](./Playbook.md).
- [ ] **Deep Work Protocol:** Establish "Golden Hours" across the team where meetings are strictly forbidden.
- [ ] **Weekly Sync:** Replace status updates with a 15-min [Weekly Review](./templates/Weekly-Review.md) focused on process blockers.

---

## 📊 Phase 2: Visibility & Measurement (2–3 months)
*Objective: Move from "feeling busy" to "knowing performance".*

- [ ] **Metric Baseline:** Start tracking Predictability (Planned vs. Done) and Lead Time.
- [ ] **Self-Assessment:** Engineers begin bi-weekly [Self-Assessments](./Self-Assessment.md) to find personal leverage points.
- [ ] **Transparency:** Share anonymized team metrics to celebrate "Focus Wins" and identify systemic bottlenecks.

---

## 🔄 Phase 3: System Optimization (3–6 months)
*Objective: Ruthless elimination of waste and continuous tuning.*

- [ ] **Workflow Refactoring:** Use gathered data to kill inefficient meetings or automate manual "to-do" steps.
- [ ] **Antifragility Drill:** Conduct a "Stress Test" (e.g., simulating a sudden change in requirements) to see how the system adapts.
- [ ] **Consistency Check:** Ensure 90% of the team hits a "Stable" score in their assessments.

---

## 🚀 Phase 4: Scaling & Cultural Lock-in (6–12 months)
*Objective: Make CRAFTER the "New Normal".*

- [ ] **Cross-Team Standardization:** Apply the same execution language across the entire engineering department.
- [ ] **Onboarding Integration:** CRAFTER OS becomes the first thing a new hire learns during their first week.
- [ ] **Systemic Resilience:** The culture is now strong enough to survive organizational changes, tight deadlines, and scaling.

---

## 📈 The North Star Metric
**A high-performance team is defined by:**
1. **High Predictability:** We ship what we promise, on time.
2. **Deep Satisfaction:** Engineers spend more time building and less time "managing chaos."
3. **Continuous Growth:** The system gets better every single month.

---
### 📝 Lead Tip
Don't rush the phases. **Phase 1 (Deep Work)** is the most critical; if the team doesn't learn to protect their focus, the metrics in Phase 2 will only measure their burnout.
